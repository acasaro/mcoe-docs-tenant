This guide walks through the process of getting your mobile app project set up in Bitrise. The MCoE team handles the heavy lifting — you just need to provide a few details.

## Prerequisites

Before starting the onboarding process, make sure:

- All team members who need access have an **approved Secure request** for `AZU_BITRISE_USERS`
- You know which **GitHub organization** hosts your mobile app repositories

<Tip>
If you've already [requested access](/developers/bitrise-platform/bitrise-access) and confirmed SSO, you're ready to proceed.
</Tip>

## What we need from you

Reach out to the MCoE team with the following:

| Item | Details |
| --- | --- |
| **GitHub organization name** | The org that hosts your mobile repositories |
| **Repository links** | Direct links to each mobile app repo |
| **CI Platform Engineer emails** | Engineers who will lead the migration (must have `AZU_BITRISE_USERS` access) |

## What happens next

Once the MCoE team has your details, we will:

<Steps>
<Step title="Connect your GitHub org">
If not already connected, we'll install the Bitrise GitHub app on your organization.
</Step>

<Step title="Create your Bitrise CI project">
We'll create the project and connect the required repositories.
</Step>

<Step title="Set up access groups">
Two groups are created per app:

- `[App Name]__OWNERS` — Platform Engineer access
- `[App Name]__CONTRIBUTORS` — Tester / QA access (onboarded later)
</Step>

<Step title="Notify your team">
You'll be notified when Bitrise CI is fully integrated and ready for migration.
</Step>
</Steps>

Post in the **Bitrise Support** channel inside MCoE on Microsoft Teams, or contact Anthony Asaro directly with the information above.
