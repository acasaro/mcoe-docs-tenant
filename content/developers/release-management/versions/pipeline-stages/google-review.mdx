The Google Review stage is the Android equivalent of the [App Store Review](/developers/release-management/versions/pipeline-stages/app-store-review) stage. Here, you submit your Android build to Google Play Console for review before releasing it to production.

<Note>To send your app to Google Play Console review, you need to have the Release Manager role for your connected app.</Note>

## Prerequisites

Before you can submit for Google Play review:

- All six approvals must be **Approved** (see [Approvals](/developers/release-management/versions/pipeline-stages/approvals))
- Your Google Play Console configuration must be complete (see [Settings](/developers/release-management/manage-rollout/settings))
- Your release candidate must have been uploaded to Google Play (see [Google Play Upload](/developers/release-management/versions/pipeline-stages/google-play-testing))

## Submitting for Review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Google Review stage">
    Navigate to your version's pipeline view and expand the Google Review stage card.

    <Frame caption="The Google Review stage in Ready for Review state">
      ![google-review-ready](/images/image-place-holder.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure release settings">
    Review the release settings section:

    - **Staged Rollout** — Toggle on/off. When enabled, your app rolls out gradually to users over 7 days in percentage-based stages instead of all at once.
    - **Release Type** — Choose between a full release to all users or a staged rollout.

    <Frame caption="Google Play release settings with staged rollout options">
      ![google-release-settings](/images/image-place-holder.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and update release notes (optional)">
    Review the release notes that will appear on the Google Play listing. You can edit these before submission — the change only affects the current release, not your default presets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for review">
    Click **"Send for Review"**. The build is submitted to Google for review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tracking Review Status

After submission, the stage card shows the current Google Play review status:

| Status | Meaning | What to Do |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **In Review** | Google is currently reviewing your app | Wait — Google reviews typically take a few hours to several days |
| **Approved** | Google has approved your app | Move to the [Google Release](/developers/release-management/versions/pipeline-stages/google-release) stage |
| **Rejected** | Google has rejected your submission | Review the rejection reason and address the issues |

## If Your App Is Rejected

If Google rejects your submission:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the rejection reason">
    Read the rejection details shown in the stage card. Google provides specific policy violations or issues with each rejection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Determine the type of fix needed">
    - **Code changes needed** — Go back to the [Release Candidate](/developers/release-management/versions/pipeline-stages/release-candidate) stage to select a new build
    - **Metadata changes only** — Update via the release notes editor
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make corrections">
    Address the issues Google identified.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-submit for review">
    Submit for review again after corrections are made.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>Common rejection reasons include: policy violations, crashes or ANRs detected during review, incomplete store listing information, and privacy policy issues.</Note>

## Troubleshooting

**"Send for Review" button not available:**
- Ensure all six approvals are in Approved status
- Verify Google Play Console configuration is complete
- Confirm the release candidate has been uploaded to Google Play

**Review taking longer than expected:**
- Google Play reviews can vary from a few hours to several days
- New apps and apps with significant changes may require longer review times
- Check Google Play Console directly for any additional information requests
