After selecting a release candidate and creating tasks for approvals, the release process of an Android app reaches the stage where it must access Google Play:

1. [Google Play upload](#uploading-the-release-candidate-to-google-play): Upload the release candidate to Google Play.
2. [Distribute for testing](#distributing-android-release-candidates-for-testing): You can distribute the release candidate to Google Play testing tracks.

Once you [selected a release candidate](/pages/rollouts/03-version-stages/02-release-candidate-stage) in Rollouts, you can continue with uploading your app to Google Play.

<Note>If you set up [auto-upload](../configuring-a-release/configuring-auto-upload) during the release configuration or as a [release preset](../release-presets), this stage will be completed automatically.</Note>


<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your release">
    Open your release.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Google Play on the left navigation bar">
    Select Google Play on the left navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="In the Upload to Google Play section, click Upload">
    ![ready-to-upload.png](/images/image-place-holder.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the upload to finish">
    Wait for the upload to finish.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Distributing Android release candidates for testing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your release and go to the Beta testing in Google Play section">
    ![testing-google-play-processing-finished.png](/images/image-place-holder.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a testing track">
    Rollout supports open, internal, and closed testing. For more information about Google Play testing, check out the [Google Play Console documentation](https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/9845334).

    <Note>
    If there is a newer build (a build with a higher `version_code` number) on the testing track, you can't replace that build with your release candidate and the **Start** button will not be available.

    If there is an older build on the testing track, your release candidate will replace it.

    ![testing-google-play-cant-replace.png](/images/image-place-holder.png)
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the Start button next to the name of your track to start the distribution">
    If all goes well, the **Status** of the build should change to **Testing**.
  </Step>
</Steps>
